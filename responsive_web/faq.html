Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a Savannah cat?

A Savannah cat is a hybrid breed, a cross between a domestic cat and a wild African serval. They are known for their tall, lean bodies, spotted coats, and energetic personalities.

2. Are Savannah cats legal everywhere?

Laws vary depending on your state, country, or even city. Some areas restrict ownership of certain generations (especially F1–F3), so always check local regulations before getting a Savannah cat.

3. What’s the difference between F1, F2, F3, etc.?

These refer to the generation — how far removed the cat is from the serval ancestor:

  • F1: 50% serval, usually the largest and wildest.
  • F2–F3: Still very exotic-looking, but more domestic in behavior.
  • F4 and beyond: Considered fully domestic by most standards.

4. Do Savannah cats make good pets?

Yes — for the right person. They're intelligent, playful, loyal, and can bond deeply with their owners. But they also require more stimulation and space than a typical house cat.

5. Do they get along with other pets?

It depends on the individual cat and how they’re raised. Many Savannahs can get along with dogs and other cats if properly socialized early on.

6. Are Savannah cats hypoallergenic?

No cat is truly hypoallergenic, but some owners with mild allergies report fewer issues with Savannahs. Still, it varies from person to person.

7. How big do Savannah cats get?

F1–F2 males can weigh 17–25+ pounds and stand up to 17 inches tall at the shoulder. Later generations are smaller, but still larger than average domestic cats.

8. Do they need special care?

Yes. They benefit from:

  • A high-protein diet (often raw or premium wet food)
  • Regular play and enrichment
  • Safe outdoor access (catios or leash walks)
  • Vet care with someone familiar with hybrids

9. Are they good with kids?

Older kids who understand boundaries usually do great with Savannahs. Younger children may need supervision, as these cats are high-energy and don’t always love being handled roughly.

10. Can Savannah cats be trained?

Absolutely! They’re smart and can learn tricks, walk on a leash, and even use puzzle feeders. Training with positive reinforcement works best.
